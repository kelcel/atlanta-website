History of Atlanta

Learn about Atlanta's rich history.

Founding

Once a village inhabited by the Creek and Cherokee Indians. Centered on the"zero milepost" for the 1836 Western and Atlantic Railroad, The settlement then called 'Thrasherville' connected the port of Savannah to the Midwest. As the outpost budded into a proper town the name was changed to "Atlanta" in 1847. As a transportation hub and manufacturing giant, Atlanta would serve a crucial role during both the Civil War and World War II.

Geography

Atlanta sits at the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains and upon the ridge for the Chattahoochee River. The city features many forests, hills, and lakes that make it easy to get acquainted with nature. Atlanta has a humid subtropical climate with four distinct seasons. The city receives abundant amounts of rainfall throughout the year.

Culture

The culture of Atlanta was once very similar of that to most cities in the South. However, as time has passed Atlanta has developed it own identity. During the 1960's Atlanta played a pivot role in the Civil Rights Movement and has stayed on the forefront of social evolution ever since. Many immigrants have made Atlanta home, leading to a rich multi-cultural influence resonanting throughout the city. Despite this, Atlanta still maintains its traditional Southern charm.
